ANIMALS MAP PLACES

KOALA

🐨 Cuddle Up with the Sleepy Koala!

Scientific Name: Phascolarctos cinereus

Origin: Australia

🌿 Preferred Habitat:

Eucalyptus forests and woodlands.

😴 Wild Abilities:

- Sleeps up to 18–22 hours a day!

- Eats only eucalyptus leaves – toxic to most animals.

- Has a special digestive system to detoxify their food.

🐨 Fun Fact: Koalas aren’t bears – they’re marsupials!

Koalas are native to Australia and are known for their adorable appearance and tree-dwelling lifestyle. They primarily eat eucalyptus leaves and are often seen sleeping in trees.

Koalas have a unique digestive system that allows them to detoxify the eucalyptus leaves they consume, which are toxic to most other animals.

🐨 Fun Fact: Koalas have fingerprints that are almost indistinguishable from human fingerprints!
