Rhino beetles are a group of beetles in the family Scarabaeidae. They are known for their large size and distinctive horns, which resemble those of rhinoceroses. These beetles are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where they inhabit forests and other wooded areas. Rhino beetles are herbivorous, feeding on tree sap, fruit, and decaying plant matter. They play an important role in their ecosystems as decomposers and pollinators. Some species of rhino beetles are also kept as pets due to their unique appearance and fascinating behavior.

Rhino beetles are known for their impressive size and strength, with some species capable of lifting objects many times their own weight. They are also known for their unique mating rituals, which often involve elaborate displays and battles between males.
